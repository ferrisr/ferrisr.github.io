A COVID-19 Long-Term Care Surveillance and Communication Platform 

Background. Long-Term Care Facilities (LTCFs) have been a long-neglected sector of the healthcare continuum. The coronavirus 2019 (COVID-19) pandemic has laid bare many of the pervasive challenges for LTCFs, including disparate and often limited resources, high staff turnover, and limited policy guidance. In March 2020 a local health department mobilized 14 employees to manage the COVID-19 response with the County’s 336 LTCFs as Liaisons. LTCFs in the County comprise of organizations with overlapping facility types, including 24 Skilled Nursing Facilities, 311 Assisted Living Facilities and three apartment units. The County needed to rapidly mobilize to collect COVID-19 case data from LTCFs and selectively communicate with outbreak and high-risk facilities. The collection of testing data in an unstandardized format, however, posed logistic and monitoring barriers in support of these settings.

Objective. Develop and implement a rapid communication and data surveillance platform for LTCF Liaisons.

Methods. We used Qualtrics – a web-based survey software – as a line list submission portal for facilities. Unique Qualtrics URLs were assigned for each facility with a universally unique identifier (UUID). Next, we built a separate web interface for Liaisons to directly monitor and message facilities based on Qualtrics submissions and facility features, including a facility’s size and outbreak status. Our Liaison web platform was created using Django, a scalable and open source web development tool. Webhooks populated survey responses from Qualtrics into the communication platform. A Simple Mail Transfer Protocol (SMTP client) was built into our platform using the smtlib Python module to communicate with facilities by email. SMS functionality was integrated into our platform using Twilio, a telecommunications software.
