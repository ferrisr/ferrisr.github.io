An Antibiotic Stewardship Dashboard for Arizona Long-term Care Facilities 

Background. Skilled nursing facility (SNF) residents are at heightened risk for antibiotic-resistant infection due to declining immune function, frequent healthcare contacts, institutional living, and selective antibiotic pressure. In 2015, several AZ facilities engaged in an innovative antimicrobial stewardship program (ASP) to reduce use of antibiotics for suspected urinary tract infections (UTI). We sought to scale the success of this pilot program to other Arizona SNFs through creation of a standard implementation package. We incorporated elements to enhance the effectiveness of our protocol, including qualitative evidence, previously published ASPs, and federal guidance documents. We further include expanded trainings and data collection processes in the context of increased prescribing due to Coronavirus Disease (COVID-19).

Objective. We aimed to develop an antimicrobial stewardship protocol by developing a semi-automated data feedback report to isolate and target tangible areas for improvement.

Methods. We used R Shiny to generate antimicrobial prescribing dashboards to help guide antibiotic prescribing in partner facilities. Data are reported by an Infection Preventionist or equivalent staff. We developed an algorithm to quantify inappropriate antibiotic prescribing using standardized prescribing criteria. Facility-wide and resident level data are aggregated into key metrics, including a sum of resident days, antibiotic orders, antibiotic days of therapy, antibiotics prescribed prior to a lab result, urine C&S and respiratory x-rays ordered meeting clinical symptoms, and total of antibiotics prescribed not meeting the standardized criteria. These data are fed into dashboards and distributed to partner facilities (Figure).
