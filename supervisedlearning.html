Unsupervised Learning of Genetic Data

Background. Genetic data has become increasingly accessible and affordable, creating vast amounts of data for researchers to analyze. However, analyzing genetic data can be challenging due to the high dimensionality and complexity of the data. Unsupervised machine learning can help by identifying patterns and relationships in genetic data that can inform genetic research and personalized medicine.

Objective. The objective of this project was to use unsupervised machine learning to cluster genetic samples based on their genomic features. By clustering genetic samples into distinct groups, researchers can identify potential genetic associations with diseases or traits and develop personalized medicine approaches.

Methods. 
1. Data collection and preprocessing: The first step is to collect genetic data from the samples of interest and preprocess it for analysis. This may involve filtering out low-quality data, normalizing the data, and selecting relevant genomic features.
2. Feature selection and extraction: Next, researchers need to identify the genomic features that will be used to cluster the genetic samples. These features could include single-nucleotide polymorphisms (SNPs), copy number variations (CNVs), or gene expression levels.
3. Clustering: Researchers can use clustering algorithms to group genetic samples with similar genomic features. Some commonly used clustering algorithms for genetic data include k-means, hierarchical clustering, and principal component analysis (PCA).
4. Evaluation and interpretation: Finally, researchers need to evaluate the quality of the clustering results and interpret the clusters. This involves determining the optimal number of clusters and visualizing the clusters to gain insights into genetic associations with diseases or traits.
