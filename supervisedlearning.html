Unsupervised Learning of an Antibody Test

Background. The development and evaluation of diagnostic tests are critical in healthcare, as they enable accurate disease diagnosis, monitoring, and treatment. One commonly used diagnostic test is the enzyme-linked immunosorbent assay (EIA), which detects the presence of antibodies or antigens in a sample. EIA tests are widely used for the diagnosis of infectious diseases, autoimmune disorders, and cancer, among others. The benefit of EIA tests lie in its ability to accurately and efficiently detect a particular antibody with a high degree of sensitivity and specificity.

Objective. In this project, we sought to isolate predictive features of a proprietary diagnostic EIA test for the detection of a specific antibody in the human body.

Methods. To evaluate the performance of the novel EIA test, we used laboratory measures such as eosinophil count, procalcitonin, C Reactive protein, and hemoglobin as potential exposures. We compared the results obtained from the EIA test with those obtained from other existing diagnostic tests. We evaluated the test's performance by analyzing the diagnostic accuracy measures, including sensitivity, specificity, positive predictive value, and negative predictive value.

We applied various dimensionality reduction methods including as Principal Component Analysis, Singular Value Decomposition (SVD), truncated-SVD, and Uniform Manifold Approximation and Projection to identify the most important features that explained the variance in our data. Reducing the number of features simplified the analysis of the EIA test's performance and improved interpretability.
