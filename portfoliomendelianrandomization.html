Association of Physical Activity with Body Composition:
A Mendelian Randomization Study

Background. Obesity has reached critical levels, and continues to exacerbate pervasive public health concerns, including cardiovascular diseases, cancers, type-2 diabetes, mental health and discrimination. This high prevalence has been ascribed to an increasingly obesogenic environment, characterized by reduced physical activity, sedentary-oriented recreational and occupational activities, and dietary options inundated by highly palatable energy-dense foods. Of particular importance for improving body composition is reducing visceral adipose tissue (VAT), which is a stronger predictor of mortality compared to non-specific anthropometric and subcutaneous fat measures.

Objective. We sought to evaluate the dose-response relationship between 1) physical activity and whole lean body mass, 2) physical activity and body mass index, 3) physical activity and waist circumference, 4) physical activity and WHR, 5) physical activity and total body fat % and 6) physical activity and visceral adipose tissue (Figure 1) using Mendelian Randomization. 

Methods. Mendelian randomization (MR) is a form of instrumental variable analysis that leverages genetic variants from genome wide association studies (GWAS) to infer causality between an exposure and a given outcome. The assignment of genetic variants during gamete formation confers a lifelong exposure for individuals randomized to a given allelic variant. Unlike exposures measured at the phenotype level, genetic variants are not typically associated with a multitude of behavioral, social, or physiological factors that may confound an epidemiological association. MR may thus avoid many of the pitfalls common to classical observational studies, including confounding and reverse causation. Both self-reported and accelerometer-measured exposures were used to evaluate PA associations with body composition measures. Mendelian randomization analyses, quality control procedures, and figures were generated using the “TwoSampleMR”, “MVMR” and "forest" R packages.
