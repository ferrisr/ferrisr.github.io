Contribution of Biologic Response Modifiers to the Risk of Coccidioidomycosis Severity

Background. Coccidioidomycosis is a fungal infection endemic to the southwestern United States and elsewhere in the Western Hemisphere. Although a small percentage of infections in otherwise healthy persons result in life-threatening respiratory failure or disseminated Coccidioidomycosis , the risk increases as much as 150-fold in immunosuppressed patients. Those receiving biological response modifiers are an emerging at-risk group for some infections. Biological response modifiers are a diverse and expanding number of medications used for autoimmune diseases.

Objective. To further explore Coccidioidomycosis risk in autoimmune patients on biological response modifiers, we conducted a retrospective cohort study in the Tucson and Phoenix areas to determine the risk posed by both autoimmune itself and these medications in patients with autoimmune.

Methods. Categorical variables are summarized using counts and percentages. Groups were compared using Fisher’s exact χ2 test. Continuous measures were assessed using the non-parametric two-sided Mann-Whitney U test for independent samples. P values <0.05 were considered significant with no correction for multiple comparisons.
